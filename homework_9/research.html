Properties of Sampling Mean and Variance

Sampling Mean

The sampling mean, denoted as \( \bar{X} \), has the following properties:

  • Unbiased Estimator: \( E[\bar{X}] = \mu \)
  • Variance Reduction: \( \text{Var}(\bar{X}) = \frac{\sigma^2}{n} \)

Sampling Variance

The sampling variance measures the variability of sample means:

  • Formula: \( \text{Var}(\bar{X}) = \frac{\sigma^2}{n} \)
  • Decreases with Sample Size: Larger \( n \) results in smaller variance.

Law of Large Numbers (LLN)

The LLN states that as the sample size \( n \) increases, the sample mean \( \bar{X} \) approaches the population mean \( \mu \).

Mathematical Representation:

Weak Law: \( P\left(\left|\bar{X} - \mu\right| > \epsilon\right) \to 0 \) as \( n \to \infty \)

Strong Law: \( \bar{X} \to \mu \) almost surely as \( n \to \infty \)

Applications in Cybersecurity

  • Anomaly Detection: Identifying deviations from normal traffic patterns.
  • Threat Intelligence: Estimating malware behavior statistics.
  • Password Security: Analyzing password distributions to identify weak passwords.
