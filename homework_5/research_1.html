Proof of the Cauchy-Schwarz Inequality

Let 𝑑 and 𝑓 be vectors in \( \mathbb{R}^n \). The Cauchy-Schwarz inequality states that:

\[ |\mathbf{a} \cdot \mathbf{b}| \leq \|\mathbf{a}\| \|\mathbf{b}\|, \]

where \( \mathbf{a} \cdot \mathbf{b} \) is the dot product of \( \mathbf{a} \) and \( \mathbf{b} \), and \( \|\mathbf{a}\| \) and \( \|\mathbf{b}\| \) are their magnitudes, given by \( \|\mathbf{a}\| = \sqrt{\mathbf{a} \cdot \mathbf{a}} \) and \( \|\mathbf{b}\| = \sqrt{\mathbf{b} \cdot \mathbf{b}} \).

Step-by-Step Proof

  1. Define a new vector involving a scalar \( t \):

    \[ \|\mathbf{a} - t \mathbf{b}\|^2 \geq 0, \]

    since the norm (or squared magnitude) of any vector is always non-negative.

  2. Expand the norm squared:

    \[ \|\mathbf{a} - t \mathbf{b}\|^2 = (\mathbf{a} - t \mathbf{b}) \cdot (\mathbf{a} - t \mathbf{b}). \]

    Using the distributive property of the dot product, this expands to

    \[ = \mathbf{a} \cdot \mathbf{a} - 2t (\mathbf{a} \cdot \mathbf{b}) + t^2 (\mathbf{b} \cdot \mathbf{b}). \]

    which simplifies to

    \[ = \|\mathbf{a}\|^2 - 2t (\mathbf{a} \cdot \mathbf{b}) + t^2 \|\mathbf{b}\|^2. \]

  3. Consider this expression as a function of \( t \):

    Define

    \[ f(t) = \|\mathbf{a}\|^2 - 2t (\mathbf{a} \cdot \mathbf{b}) + t^2 \|\mathbf{b}\|^2. \]

    Since \( \|\mathbf{a} - t \mathbf{b}\|^2 \geq 0 \), it follows that \( f(t) \geq 0 \) for all real \( t \).

  4. Find the minimum of \( f(t) \):

    Since \( f(t) \) is a quadratic function in \( t \), it reaches its minimum where the derivative with respect to \( t \) is zero. Differentiate \( f(t) \) with respect to \( t \):

    \[ f'(t) = -2 (\mathbf{a} \cdot \mathbf{b}) + 2t \|\mathbf{b}\|^2. \]

    Setting \( f'(t) = 0 \), we find

    \[ t = \frac{\mathbf{a} \cdot \mathbf{b}}{\|\mathbf{b}\|^2}. \]

  5. Substitute \( t \) back into \( f(t) \):

    Substitute \( t = \frac{\mathbf{a} \cdot \mathbf{b}}{\|\mathbf{b}\|^2} \) into \( f(t) \):

    \[ f\left(\frac{\mathbf{a} \cdot \mathbf{b}}{\|\mathbf{b}\|^2}\right) = \|\mathbf{a}\|^2 - 2 \frac{(\mathbf{a} \cdot \mathbf{b})^2}{\|\mathbf{b}\|^2} + \frac{(\mathbf{a} \cdot \mathbf{b})^2}{\|\mathbf{b}\|^2}. \]

    Simplifying, we get

    \[ = \|\mathbf{a}\|^2 - \frac{(\mathbf{a} \cdot \mathbf{b})^2}{\|\mathbf{b}\|^2}. \]

  6. Non-negativity implies the inequality:

    Since \( f(t) \geq 0 \), we have

    \[ \|\mathbf{a}\|^2 - \frac{(\mathbf{a} \cdot \mathbf{b})^2}{\|\mathbf{b}\|^2} \geq 0. \]

    Rearranging, this gives

    \[ (\mathbf{a} \cdot \mathbf{b})^2 \leq \|\mathbf{a}\|^2 \|\mathbf{b}\|^2. \]

    Taking the square root of both sides (and noting that both sides are non-negative), we obtain the Cauchy-Schwarz inequality:

    \[ |\mathbf{a} \cdot \mathbf{b}| \leq \|\mathbf{a}\| \|\mathbf{b}\|. \]

This completes the proof.
